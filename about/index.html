About Me

TL;DR:

I have a degree in Music Business, but fell in love with programming during my last year in college. In the past few years, I have worked at all three major labels, Nielsen and Condé Nast. I have recently quit my job to pursue a career in software development.

OPENING SONATA

Born and raised in Shenzhen (check this video out), I moved to New York in 2012 for college. Having a strong passion in music and knowing how difficult it is to make a decent living as a musician, I majored in Music Business and fought my way into the “The Big Three”: Warner, Sony and Universal. Fun work, no dress code, free CDs and vinyl records, occasional artist performances. Had a blast.

ADAGIO

During my last year at NYU, I interned at Nielsen and had my ‘love at first sight’ moment. Not necessarily the best kind - I was writing basic HTML and tweaking CSS templates for newsletters. Nevertheless, a door to a new world was opened: a world full of logical operators and frustration in text alignment. I immediately declared a minor in Web Programming and Applications afterwards, learned Python, SQL, JavaScript, PHP, Processing and MongoDB (really need a refresher on this one) and was pleasantly surprised by the fact that learning how to program really satisfied my curious mind and more importantly, gave me a sense of purpose.

MINUET AND TRIO

Right after graduation, I landed a job at Condé Nast in the beautiful 1 World Trade Center office as an Ad Ops Specialist. Being in Ad Ops was similar to being a bridge between engineering and sales - the two teams speak very different languages and I had to learn to be well-versed in both. I was living in the browser console 24/7, implementing customized code for rich media units (a term for fancy ads), and making sure the sales team have full ad tech support(and explaining to them how web pages work, at times.#adopsjoke). At this job, I realized that I truly enjoyed working with the engineering team, QA-ing code and fixing websites, and was not really interested in the sales side of the business. I was ready and simply couldn’t wait to dive into the world of software development.

ALLEGRO

Here I am, ready to code more, workout more (who am I kidding) and explore more. Nobody says a four-year CS education is required to start a career in engineering.
It takes gumption, a lot of it.
