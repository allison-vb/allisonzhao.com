About Me

TL;DR:

I have a degree in Music Business, but fell in love with programming during my last year in college. In the past few years, I have worked at all three major record labels, Nielsen and Condé Nast. I am now a fullstack software developer.

OPENING SONATA

Born and raised in Shenzhen (check this video out), I moved to New York in 2012 for college. Having a strong passion in music and knowing how difficult it is to make a decent living as a musician, I majored in Music Business and fought my way into the “The Big Three”: Warner, Sony and Universal. Fun work, no dress code, free CDs and vinyl records, occasional artist performances in the office. Had a blast.

ADAGIO

During my last year at NYU, I interned at Nielsen and had my ‘love at first sight’ moment. Not necessarily the best kind - I was writing basic HTML and tweaking CSS templates for newsletters. Nevertheless, a door to a new world was opened: a world full of logical operators and frustration in text alignment. I immediately declared a minor in Web Programming and Applications afterwards, learned Python, SQL, JavaScript, PHP, Processing and MongoDB (really need a refresher on this one) and was pleasantly surprised by the fact that learning how to program really satisfied my curious mind and more importantly, gave me a sense of purpose.

MINUET AND TRIO

Right after graduation, I landed a job at Condé Nast in the beautiful 1 World Trade Center office as an Ad Ops Specialist. Working in Ad Ops was similar to building a bridge between engineering and sales - the two teams speak very different languages and I had to learn to be well-versed in both. Through living in the browser console 24/7, I realized that I truly enjoyed learning about how web development works under the hood, and decided to pursue software engineering full-time.

ALLEGRO

Here I am, ready to code more, learn more and experience more (hopefully workout more but who am I kidding). Nobody says a four-year CS education is required to start a career in engineering.
It takes gumption, a lot of it.
